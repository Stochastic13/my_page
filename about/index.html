My current home 5 years before I was born. A must read perspective by Carl Sagan Hi! I am Satwik, a medical (MBBS) graduate from AIIMS, New Delhi . I am currently on a curiosity-fuelled exploration of Computational Neuroscience, Brain-Computer Interfaces and AI. I am interested in all things science (learning, teaching (if I can) and simply witnessing!), literature and all new things. You can find my projects, publications and thoughts on random topics on this website.

Interests

Dissecting the above umbrella terms further, I am interested in Neuronal Modeling at network scale to study neural computation, learning and also neural coding (how information translates fromt he external world to that of neural representations). I am especially interested in the parallels between AI-based models and neuronal models in what they can teach about the way our brains function; reverse-engineering the brain! I think AI has the promise of the next big revolution in biology (or rather, in everything)! I believe that such inquiries along with the progress in BCI technologies (another interest of mine) can greatly further our way towards a sci-fi worthy future with cool brain gadgets, ultra-efficient medical and psychiatric therapeutics and ultimately, a true understanding of how the brain works!

Research Experience

I have previously worked on data-analytic projects in a wide array of fields including clustering for unsupervised sub-phenotype discovery in a pediatric asthma cohort Under the department of Pediatrics, AIIMS, New Delhi (and comparing the performance of clustering algorithms on clinical data), dynamical simulation of an oncogenically relevant genetic network Under the Center for BioSystems Science and Engineering, IISc, Bangalore , bayesian integrative modeling of macromolecular assemblies, gradient-free stochastic optimization of parameters in integrative modeling Under the Integrative Structural Biology lab, NCBS, Bangalore , transformer based self-supervised learning frameworks for ECG data and deep learning frameworks for predicting bone-age from radiographs Under the Computational Biology and ML lab, Yale, Connecticut . I have further attended the Computational Approaches to Memory and Plasticity (CAMP) workshop (computational neuroscience from the single-network biophysical modeling to multi-neuron network dynamics simulations) at NCBS in 2018, and the Simons Monsoon School (physics of life; physics and mathamtics in biology) at NCBS in 2018. Further details in the CV!
